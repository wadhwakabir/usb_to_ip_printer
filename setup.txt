======================================================================
                   USB Print Bridge - Setup Guide
======================================================================

This little box turns your USB printer into a wireless printer.
You only have to set it up once. No apps to install.


WHAT YOU NEED
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  * The print bridge (the small board in the enclosure)
  * Its power cable (USB-C)
  * A USB cable from the bridge to your printer
  * Your home Wi-Fi name and password
  * A phone, and a computer to print from


STEP 1: PLUG IT IN
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  1. Plug the USB cable from the bridge's HOST port into
     your printer's USB port.
  2. Turn the printer on.
  3. Plug the bridge's power cable in.

  The little light on the bridge will do the following:
      WHITE   ...  starting up        (a few seconds)
      PURPLE  ...  waiting for Wi-Fi  (blinks)
      ORANGE  ...  on Wi-Fi, no printer yet
      GREEN   ...  ready to print

  First time you plug it in, you'll see PURPLE blinking.
  That means it's waiting for you to tell it which Wi-Fi to join.


STEP 2: TELL IT YOUR WI-FI (do this from your phone)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  1. On your phone, open Wi-Fi settings.

  2. Connect to the network called:

          esp32-printer

     Password:

          printprint

  3. A setup page should pop up automatically saying
     "Sign in to network" or similar. Tap it.

     If nothing pops up, open any web browser and go to:

          http://192.168.4.1

  4. On the setup page:
       - Pick YOUR home Wi-Fi name from the list
         (or type it in)
       - Type your home Wi-Fi password
       - Tap "Save and reboot"

  5. The bridge will restart. Your phone will leave the
     "esp32-printer" network on its own.

  6. Watch the light on the bridge:
       - PURPLE blinking means it didn't connect.
         Check the password and try again.
       - GREEN solid means you're done with this step.
       - ORANGE blinking means it joined Wi-Fi but can't
         see the printer. Check the USB cable is plugged
         all the way in, and the printer is on.

  You only do this once. The bridge remembers your Wi-Fi.


STEP 3: ADD THE PRINTER ON YOUR COMPUTER
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  --- On a Mac --------------------------------------------------

  1. Open System Settings -> Printers & Scanners.
  2. Click "Add Printer, Scanner, or Fax..."
  3. Click the IP tab (the globe icon at the top).
  4. Fill it in exactly like this:

       Address:   esp32-printer.local
       Protocol:  HP Jetdirect - Socket
       Queue:     (leave empty)
       Name:      (anything you want, e.g. "Home Printer")
       Use:       pick your printer model from the list

  5. Click Add.
  6. Print a test page from any app (File -> Print).


  --- On Windows 10 or 11 ---------------------------------------

  1. Settings -> Bluetooth & devices -> Printers & scanners.
  2. Click "Add device", then after a moment click
     "Add manually".
  3. Choose "Add a printer using a TCP/IP address or hostname".
     Click Next.
  4. Fill in:

       Device type:  TCP/IP Device
       Hostname:     esp32-printer.local
       Port name:    (fills in automatically)

     UNCHECK "Query the printer and automatically select
     the driver to use". Click Next.
  5. If it asks for "Device Type", pick:

       Custom -> Settings

     Set:
       Protocol:     Raw
       Port Number:  9100

     Click OK, then Next.
  6. Pick your printer model from the list. If it's not
     there, install the driver from your printer-maker's
     website first, then come back here.
  7. Give it a name and finish.
  8. Print a test page.


  If "esp32-printer.local" doesn't work on Windows, use the
  bridge's IP address instead. See "Finding the IP" below.


STEP 4: PRINT
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Just print from any app, like you would to any printer.
  Pick "Home Printer" (or whatever you named it) from the
  printer list.

  While printing:
      YELLOW blinking  ...  receiving your print job
      GREEN  blinking  ...  finished, all good


TROUBLESHOOTING
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Light is ORANGE blinking
      The bridge is on Wi-Fi but can't see the printer.
      - Is the USB cable plugged all the way into both ends?
      - Is the printer turned on?
      - Give it 30 seconds; some printers take a while to
        wake up.
      - Try unplugging the printer's USB cable and plugging
        it back in.

  Light is PURPLE blinking and won't go away
      The bridge couldn't join your home Wi-Fi.
      Connect to "esp32-printer" on your phone again and
      re-enter the password (see Step 2).

  Light is RED blinking
      Something went wrong with the printer mid-job.
      1. Turn the printer off and on.
      2. Unplug and replug the USB cable to the bridge.
      3. Wait for the light to go back to GREEN, then
         try printing again.

  "esp32-printer.local" can't be found
      Some networks don't support .local names.
      See "Finding the IP" below and use the number
      instead (looks like 192.168.1.42).

  It worked before, now it's PURPLE blinking
      The bridge lost Wi-Fi. This can happen if:
      - Your Wi-Fi password changed: reconnect to
        "esp32-printer" (Step 2) and enter the new one.
      - Your router was off for too long: the bridge will
        usually recover on its own within 2 minutes; if
        not, unplug the bridge and plug it back in.


FINDING THE IP (only if .local doesn't work)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Log into your home router's admin page (usually
  http://192.168.1.1 or http://192.168.0.1). Look for a
  list of connected devices. Find the one called
  "esp32-printer" and note its IP address (looks like
  192.168.1.42). Use that instead of "esp32-printer.local"
  when adding the printer.


MOVING TO A NEW HOUSE (or new Wi-Fi)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Plug the bridge in at the new place. When it can't find
  the old Wi-Fi, it will go back to PURPLE blinking and
  start its own "esp32-printer" network again.

  Just do Step 2 with the new Wi-Fi details.


GIVING UP AND ASKING FOR HELP
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  If nothing works, unplug the bridge for 10 seconds,
  plug it back in, and try Step 2 again. If you still
  can't get it working, take a photo of the light color
  and send it to whoever set this up for you.
