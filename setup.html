Print Bridge

Your USB printer, now wireless. One-time setup, then just print.

1 Plug it in

  1. Connect the USB cable from the bridge's HOST port to your printer.
  2. Turn the printer on, then plug in the bridge's power cable (USB-C).

Light blinks purple = ready for Wi-Fi setup.

2 Connect to Wi-Fi (from your phone)

  1. On your phone, join Wi-Fi: esp32-printer   password: printprint
  2. A "Sign in to network" page pops up. If not, go to http://192.168.4.1
  3. Pick your home Wi-Fi, enter password, tap Save & reboot.

Bridge restarts → light turns solid green = connected. You only do this once.

3 Add printer to your computer

macOS

  1. System Settings → Printers & Scanners → +
  2. IP tab → Address: esp32-printer.local
  3. Protocol: HP Jetdirect – Socket
  4. Use: your printer model → Add

Windows 10 / 11

  1. Settings → Printers & scanners → Add manually
  2. "Add using TCP/IP address or hostname"
  3. Hostname: esp32-printer.local
  4. Protocol: Raw   Port: 9100 → pick driver
If esp32-printer.local doesn't work, check your router's device list for esp32-printer and use its IP address.

4 Print!

Done. Print from any app — pick the printer you just added.

LED Reference

WhiteStarting up
Blue blinkConnecting to Wi-Fi
Purple blinkSetup mode (needs credentials)
Orange blinkOn Wi-Fi, no printer (check USB)
Green solidReady to print
Cyan solidComputer connected
Yellow blinkPrinting
Green blinkJob done
Red blinkError

Troubleshooting

Purple won't stop

Reconnect to esp32-printer and re-enter password.

Stuck orange

Check USB cable is seated. Printer on? Wait 30-60s.

Red blink

Turn printer off/on, reseat USB, try again.

New house?

Just plug in. Purple returns → do Step 2 with new Wi-Fi.
